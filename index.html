About Asmara

Asmara is the capital city of Eritrea, known for its modernist architecture, Italian influence, and rich history. Located in the Eritrean Highlands, it is a UNESCO World Heritage site famous for its urban planning and unique structures.

Main Attractions

Fiat Tagliero Building

The Fiat Tagliero Building is a futuristic service station designed to resemble an airplane. Built in 1938, it stands as a testament to the bold architectural vision of the Italian colonial era. Today, it is one of the most iconic landmarks in Asmara.

Asmara Cathedral

The Asmara Cathedral, also known as Cattedrale di Asmara, was built in 1922 and showcases Romanesque Revival architecture. The bell tower is a landmark in the city’s skyline, and the cathedral remains an active place of worship.

Cinema Impero

Cinema Impero is one of the finest examples of Art Deco architecture in the world. Built in 1937, the cinema is still in use today, and its preserved condition offers a window into the grandeur of Asmara’s golden age.

National Museum of Eritrea

The National Museum of Eritrea is dedicated to the history, culture, and archaeological heritage of Eritrea. The museum houses important artifacts, including ancient tools, manuscripts, and relics from the Axumite and pre-Axumite periods.
